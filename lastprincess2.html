It was the first time they had met since that fateful day in the gardens, and Zainab's heart was pounding with excitement and anticipation. Raza looked at Zainab with a mixture of admiration and skepticism. He had seen many wealthy and powerful people express support for their cause, only to abandon it at the first sign of danger. He wondered if Zainab was truly committed to the cause, or if she was just seeking a temporary thrill.


"What can you do for us?" Raza asked, his eyes fixed on Zainab.


"I can help you," Zainab replied, her voice filled with determination. "I know the layout of the city, and I have connections in the British administration. I can provide you with information and help you plan your attacks."


Raza nodded, impressed by Zainab's confidence and intelligence. "That could be useful," he said. "But we need to be careful. If we are caught, it could mean the end of our rebellion."


She understood the risks, but she was determined to see this through. She had always been a rebel at heart, and now she finally had a cause to fight for. She had grown up in a world of privilege and luxury, but she knew that her people were suffering under the British rule. She could no longer remain silent.


Together, both hatched a plan. They would launch a surprise attack on a British outpost on the outskirts of the city. Zainab would provide them with information about the outpost's defenses and the number of soldiers stationed there. Raza and his men would then launch a surprise attack, using the element of surprise to overwhelm the British soldiers.


Zainab was nervous but excited. She had never been involved in anything like this before, but she knew that it was the right thing to do. She felt alive for the first time in her life, as if she had finally found her true calling.


As they prepared for the attack, Zainab and Raza grew closer. They talked about their hopes and dreams, and their shared desire for freedom and justice. Zainab felt a deep connection to Raza, and she knew that their paths were intertwined.


The day of the attack arrived, and Zainab felt a sense of exhilaration as she watched Raza and his men launch their assault on the British outpost. She watched from a safe distance, but her heart was under fear. She knew that this was just the beginning of their fight for freedom, and more harsh battles are yet to come.
